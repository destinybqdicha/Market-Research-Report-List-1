<p><p>The aluminized steel market consists of two primary types based on the coating composition. First, aluminized steel coated with a thin layer of aluminum/silicon alloy combines the benefits of aluminum's corrosion resistance with silicon's enhanced durability, making it suitable for high-temperature applications. Second, steel coated with pure aluminum offers superior resistance to oxidation and good reflective properties, making it ideal for applications requiring high thermal resistance and aesthetic appeal. Both types are widely used across automotive, construction, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
