<p><p>Radiation detection in the industrial and scientific markets involves several key technologies. Geiger counters are widely used for their simplicity in measuring ionizing radiation levels. Scintillation detectors utilize materials that emit light when they interact with radiation, offering high sensitivity and energy discrimination. Solid-state detectors rely on semiconductor materials to directly convert radiation into electronic signals, providing high resolution and reliability. Each type serves specific applications, from safety monitoring in industrial settings to advanced research in laboratories.</p></p>
